Quality engineering in Automotive Industry

What does a quality engineer do in automotive industry?

Did you imagine how many parts are in a car? Do you know how many problems can occour with a single part? How can an OEM ensure that the car owners don't get a piece of shit for their money?

To ensure good product quality companies started employing quality enginners.They are responsible to avoid that wrong parts could reach the customers.
They don't check every features on a single part (but there are some features what have to check in 100% for sure).
Their methods make the production able to use statistics and controls where you do not have to measure all parts but you can be sure that all part is good.
A problem in the field can lead to customer claim and an OEM can loss a lot of money on it.
Ensuring same quality cost money, but it is more less than some claim costs.
Nowadays everythinh is around safety and money. To ensure a new development is enough safety OEMs and suppliers make a lot of test. No one want to see a death caused by a vehicle.

Typical types of quality engineers

Customer Quality Engineers

They are responsible for communication with the customer from aspect of quality. They have to ensure the custumer satisfaction, their tasks based on the customers request.
In the organization they are the "buyer" before the "real" customer (who decide that are they satisfied with the quality of the choosen product)

Operations Quality Engineers

Supplier Quality Engineer - Supplier Quality Developer

Quality Managers

Usually they manage all of the Quality Engineers, they always know who is the responsible for a task, who can solve the problem.
